**Escape the Backrooms** is a first-person horror survival game based on the classic internet horror legend "The Backrooms." Players find themselves trapped in an endless maze of yellow wallpaper and fluorescent-lit office spaces, where they must explore, solve puzzles, avoid dangerous entities, and find a way to escape.

- **üåå Infinite Maze Exploration**: Each level features procedurally generated mazes filled with unknown dangers

- **üß© Puzzle Mechanics**: Collect items, solve password puzzles, activate circuit breakers, and avoid traps
- **üí° Sanity System**: Manage your sanity meter - low sanity causes vision blur and hallucinations
- **üë• Multiplayer Co-op**: Support for up to 4 players to explore and survive together

## üéØ Game Guides

### Beginner Tips

1. **Start with Level 0**: This is the tutorial level where you learn basic mechanics
2. **Master the "Right-Hand Rule"**: Follow the right wall in mazes to avoid getting lost
3. **Collect Items**: Flashlights, Almond Water, Energy Bars, and other items are crucial
4. **Manage Sanity**: Watch the brain icon and replenish with Almond Water when needed
5. **Learn Entities**: Study each entity's behavior patterns and counter-strategies

### Advanced Tips

- **Adjust Gamma Settings**: Turn Gamma up to 60% or higher in dark areas
- **Graphics Settings Trick**: Setting graphics to "Lowest" gives you "night vision" in dark areas
- **Infinite Stamina Tricks**: Level 0 and certain chase sequences have infinite stamina
- **Door Trapping**: Some entities can't open doors - use this to trap them
- **Multiplayer Coordination**: Divide tasks with teammates to increase survival rate

### Entity Counter-Strategies

- **Bacteria**: Appears in Level 0, can't catch up to you, just keep moving
- **Skin-Stealer**: Can't open doors, lure into a room and close the door to trap
- **Smiler**: In chase sequences, keep running and don't look back
- **Hound**: Shine flashlight to temporarily blind it
- **Partygoers**: Use stealth tactics, crouch walk, use tables and obstacles

## ‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Game Mechanics

**Q: How do I restore sanity?**  
A: Drink Almond Water or use Medicine to restore sanity. Level 37 automatically restores sanity.

**Q: How do I find the exit?**  
A: Each level has specific objectives like collecting items, solving puzzles, or activating switches. Complete the objectives to open the exit.

**Q: How do I play multiplayer?**  
A: Select "Multiplayer" from the main menu, create or join a room. Supports up to 4 players.

**Q: How do I avoid entities?**  
A: Different entities require different strategies. Some can be trapped, some need to be avoided, some can be repelled with flashlights.

### Technical Issues

**Q: The game is too dark, I can't see anything.**  
A: Adjust Gamma settings to 60% or higher, or set graphics to "Lowest" for night vision effect.

**Q: My microphone isn't working.**  
A: Check in-game voice settings, ensure microphone permissions are granted, or use external voice software like Discord.

**Q: How do I improve FPS?**  
A: Lower graphics settings, turn off unnecessary effects, update your graphics drivers.

### Walkthrough Questions

**Q: Where are the ladder fragments in Level 0?**  
A: Use the "Right-Hand Rule" and follow the right wall. Ladder fragments are usually near the main intersection.

**Q: What is the elevator code for Level 1?**  
A: The code is randomized. Check the sticky note inside the elevator or try common combinations (e.g., Red, Red, Red, Blue).

**Q: How do I unlock The Hub?**  
A: In Level 1, find the computer password puzzle, solve it, then run the DOOR.EXE program to unlock it.

**Q: What does Moth Jelly do in Level 5?**  
A: Moth Jelly restores approximately 40% sanity and provides 11 seconds of infinite stamina and speed boost.
